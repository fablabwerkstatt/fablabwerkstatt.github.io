Basics of Lasering

In this section you will be learning the basics of 2D design, Pattern formation, and operating laser machines for cutting and engraving.


Only Two people can be there in the laser machine. If two person a lasering rest must be seated and working on their design in the big table area.

Definition

Imagine using a magic light beam that's as thin as a hair and super focused, just like a superhero laser, to cut through stuff like a breeze through your hair! But instead of chopping veggies, it's slicing through stuff like paper, wood, or even metal, making amazing patterns and shapes. Think of a computer as your bossy but super-smart friend, telling the laser exactly where to go, like drawing with a flashlight in the dark. Once it's done, ta-da! Perfect cuts, perfect shapes, just like magic. It's like having a superpower for making cool stuff, from big ships to tiny jewelry that sparkles.

Function of Laser Machine

  • Laser Beam: Diameter 0.1 - 0.3 mm; Power 1-3 kW (adjusted depending on the material)
  • Laser Resonator (glass tube filled with CO2, nitrogen, or helium) generates laser beam
  • The laser beam is directed into the cutting head via mirrors
  • Cutting head: Laser beams are focused by a focusing lens
  • The beam passes through a nozzle into which compressed gas is blown (e.g. nitrogen/oxygen)
  • Gas blows away the smoke (also called Air-Assist).
  • Additionally, particles from the cutting gap are blown away.
  • The focal point must be properly adjusted so that the laser can cut.

Laser cutting

Laser cutting is like using a special superpower light to cut through stuff without even touching it! It's perfect for making all sorts of cool shapes and designs on things. The laser beam is like a magic wand, melting, vaporizing, or burning away parts of the material to make the cuts. And the best part? It's all done without even touching the object directly! So, you can create amazing things without worrying about damaging them.

Laser Engraving

Laser engraving is like drawing with a super-precise laser pen that makes permanent marks on stuff! Instead of just cutting, it carves pictures, designs, even barcodes or serial numbers, onto things like wood, plastic, metal, or leather. It's like giving your favorite things a special tattoo that lasts forever!

DANGERS of WORKING with Laser Machines!

It is very important to follow certain rules while working with laser machines.

Fire

  • The base material is vaporized, generating significant heat
  • Some materials may catch fire during cutting
  • Dirt and deposits can cause fires
  • Be Cautious and take a help of your teacher in laser room without any shame!

Dangerous fumes and exhaust gases

  • When cutting certain materials, laser-generated airborne contaminants (LGACs) can be produced (health risk!)
  • Examples: Benzene, toluene, hydrochloric acid, isocyanates
  • Exhaust systems and/or filter systems are required to vent air outside
  • They must be properly installed and meet all manufacturer specifications
  • Particle contamination

Additional Risks

  • Eye damage from direct laser radiation or scattered radiation (visible and invisible!).
  • Loss of vision.
  • Irreparable damage.
  • Damage to skin and other tissue types.
  • Sharp edges (cutting injuries).
  • Electrical hazard Crushing danger.

Design in Inkscape

For the the sake of learning today we are going to design a key chain of your name or any other text of your liking using Inkscape.


Please follow the tutorial as it is made simplified for you to follow.

Step 1: Open the inkscape and you will find the document look like as in figure.

Step 2: Change the page orientation. Go to File > Document Properties > Make the format 1920x1080 and Orientation Landscape.

Step 3: Add the text of your choice from the side menu bar. Now write the text and size of the text can be changed from toolbar. Select font which you like your key chain to look like. For this tutorial we are using Gill Sans Ultra Bold font size 200.

Step 4: Copy the same text below as shown.

Step 5: In the second image add the offset. For that go to Path > click offset repeat the same action x10 times or more until you find the shadow of your text.

Step 6: You must have the shadow of your image like this ->

Step 7: In the second image after I change the color you can see the infill and stroke. We need only the stroke and infill is not needed

Step 8:To delete the infill click on the image and right click and find fill and stroke in the dialog box.

Step 9: In the Fill menu on side bar click on X and infill will be deleted.

Step 10: Create the node around borders of the second image by going to the side toolbar. Check the image.


This step helps you to remove unwanted lines and disconnection between the lines. MAKE SURE THE LINES ARE CONNECTED. If there is a breakage in line the cutting or engraving will not function.

Step 11: Place the name in border and adjust the position.

Step 12: To improve our process of lasering. It is recommended to change the color of your design. So change the color of the name to any color of your choice doesn't matter until both outside and inside colors are different.

Step 13: You can see the stroke around name we don't want that. Repeat step 7 and go to Strokes. Click on X in the strokes and now you only have one filling of color for smooth engraving.

Step 14: Time to create a hole to insert keys. The hole can be of your choice I am making them square. Draw a square rectangle as shown in the figure.

Step 15: Click on the rectangle and change infill and stroke for the image. Delete the infill and click on stroke you must see a rectangle with outline only. Adjust the placement of the rectangle and it should be okay according to your wish.

Step 16: Select every component of image and we are ready for lasering.
