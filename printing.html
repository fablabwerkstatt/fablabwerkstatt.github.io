How does designing work?


Design thinking typically involves five key stages:
  • Empathise: Understand the users' needs, wants, and limitations by gathering information through research and observation.
  • Define: Spot, frame, and fully understand the problem at hand.
  • Ideate: Create a list of possible solutions.
  • Prototype: Create prototypes in CAD programs of the most promising ideas.
  • Test: Gather feedback from users and use it to improve the design further.
Design cycle we must follow for 3D designing:

Always have an idea before you start designing anything on paper. Idea could be from anywhere it must inspire you to keep the project going. Followed by research on your design like how it will look in the end, what can be done to make it work and questions like that. When you are pleased with what you want to make please open your Tinkercad and start your design. Don't forget to google or ask your facilitators in the Fab Lab to help you. Once the design is ready it is important to load your design in Prusa slicer and make it understandable for the printers. Once you sliced your design and got the GCODE. Copy the file in SD card and print your design using Prusa printer. In case you are not satisfied with your design go back to research now research the design that you made and printed and where things went wrong. Again re-design, re-slice and re-print. These process will go on until you are satisfied with the final output.

Slicing


Please go through this website to learn more about slicing and how it can help you 3D printing your design.
Things to notice while slicing your design:
  • Print Settings: 0.30mm Draft (for fast prototype)
  • Filament: PLA
  • printer: original Prusa i3 MK3S and MK3S+ \ original Prusa MINI or MINI+
  • Support: Always decide based on your design whether it has any hanging object or outward design with 45 Deg angle.
  • Infill: 5%
  • Brim (NEVER FORGET)
  • Change position of object to reduce print timing
  • Resize your design
  • Slice
  • Make GCODE
