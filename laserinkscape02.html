Design in Inkscape

For the the sake of learning today we are going to design a key chain of your name or any other text of your liking using Inkscape.


Please follow the tutorial as it is made simplified for you to follow.

Step 1: Change the page orientation. Go to File > Document Properties > Make the format 1920x1080 and Orientation Landscape.

Step 2: Add the text of your choice from the side menu bar. Now write the text and size of the text can be changed from toolbar. Select font which you like your key chain to look like. For this tutorial we are using Gill Sans Ultra Bold font size 200.

Step 3: Copy the text and paste it below in the free space of your document as shown in the figure below.

Step 4: Add the offset. For that go to Path > click offset. Repeat the offset action x10 times or more until you find the shadow of your text. As shown below.

Step 5: You must repeat the change offset option so you get the shadow of your text as shown in figure.

Step 6: In shadow image change the color. You can see red color is infill and black ooutline is stroke. You only need outside black color stroke.

Step 7:To delete the infill click on the shadow image and right click and find fill and stroke in the dialog box.

Step 8: In the Fill menu on side bar click on and infill will be deleted.

Step 9: Create the node around borders of the second image by going to the side toolbar. Check the image.


This step helps you to remove unwanted lines and disconnection between the lines. MAKE SURE THE LINES ARE CONNECTED. If there is a breakage in line the cutting or engraving will not function.

Step 10: Drag the original name which we created earlier and place on the border we created.

Step 11: PLEASE make different colors for inside text and outside text

Step 12: You can see the stroke around name we don't want that. Right click on the image go to Fill and Stroke. Now click on in Strokes. Voila! You are almost there.

Step 13: Click on the rectangle shape and draw the hole inside the border.

Step 14: Change infill and stroke for the image. Delete the infill and click on stroke you must see a rectangle with outline only. Adjust the placement of the rectangle so it leave atleast 1mm to 2mm as the border space.

Step 15: Click and Select every component of image and we are ready for lasering.
